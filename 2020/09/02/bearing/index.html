Bearing

I wanted to print a real model this time. However, I’d want to paint it, and I’m not experienced enough with that yet. I’ll sand, prime and paint my N64 logo first as a test.

So instead I decided to do a mechanical print-in-place model, the 5 internal gears. I’m only going to use the defaults for this at a reasonable quality.

It seems that I’m still getting a bit of an elephant’s foot. Since the temperatures are so low now, I believe the Z offset is too small. Now that I have good adhesion I can afford to raise the nozzle a little next time.

Unfortunately, I could not get this to budge! I’m pretty sure this is because the lower layers are bound together because they were squashed together. Next time I’ll raise the Z offset until it just still adheres…

Settings

Setting Value
Preset basis Cura dynamic (0.16mm)
Bed temperature (first layer) 50°
Bed temperature 45°
