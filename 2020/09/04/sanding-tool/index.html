Sanding Tool

I have a big roll of sandpaper, I could do with something to hold it with to clean up my prints. I looked on thingiverse and found something ideal. However, it’s way too small, this is more like a nail file. It’s customisable though. But the Thingiverse customiser is so slow! And once I was done I had to wait for it to finish processing, and it never did. The Thingiverse with is having some real issues recently. So I decided to design my own in Fusion360.

Design

\I kept close to the original design on Thingiverse, but wanted it to fit nicely in the hand and also fit to a standard size of the roll of sandpaper. This meant moving the screws to side-by-side, and having to design threaded bolts and their holes. I gave them some tactile bumps and offset the faces/used a bevel to give them some room internally.

I printed the screws first. They printed nicely, although maybe I should have used concentric top layers to make them look nicer.

The plate curled and bent along the longest edge. I realised I shouldn’t be lowering the temperature so much. 5°C is probably too much, maximum 2°C, but maybe I don’t actually need to do this anymore, since it’s not the cause of the elephant’s foot. The bend in this case is not really a problem though, as it’s in the other axis and will still clamp the paper.

I then printed the body. I used a larger layer size, faster print speed, and tightened the belts more. I also tried out ironing, as I had a bit flat surface (although it won’t really be seen when in use). I’m keeping the Z offset, but using a thicker first layer.

I’m very happy with these settings! The ironed surface is so smooth. There’s also no warping, although again, it’s difficult to remove all of the brim. The screws fit nicely, and it all fits together.

I also made a quick and dirty sandpaper slicer. The width of my tool is exactly half the width of the sandpaper roll, so with this I can use my knife to make perfectly sized strips.

I tested it out on my Nintendo 64 logo and it’s really helpful. Overall, a success.
