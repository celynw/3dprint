2020-08-31

3D Printer

My new 3D printer (Creality Ender 3 V2) arrived today! It only took 5 days to arrive, from CZ. This weekend is a long one due to the bank holiday - time to build!

Construction

I set it up very carfeully following YouTube videos. It was difficult to tell which way I had to tighten the concentric casters, but everything went smoothly.

First print

I decided to print a file from the SD card, and timelapse this historic moment. I had already set up Octoprint on my Rapsberry Pi, and so I tried to print the example cat file. 5 seconds in, it aborted. Why??

Octoprint noticed that the connection was lost. I tried it a few times and got the same results. It always fails in exactly the same places. I tried everything! I was eventually somehow able to print a Benchy, but how can I stop this from happening again?

It took 2 hours. Yes, I watched it almost the entire time! (Partly because it was 2am and I needed it to finish so I could sleep)

2020-08-31

Fixing Printer

I need to fix the rebooting problem, unless I want to risk prints failing partway through.

Movement too restricted?

No, I put it together very carefully and there’s no sign this is happening.

Corrupted SD card?

No, I tried a few models from various places, and different slicers, and get the same kind of problem.

Raspberry Pi causing it?

No, I tried printing from the SD card in isolation, or from my PC with the USB cable. However, I did find that the USB connection cable causes some weird issues. Turning off the printer, and even unplugging it, it continues to be powered somehow. I had to cut the power wire out of the cable to prevent this from happening.

Power problems?

No, I tried many sockets, examining the internal connections, even powering off other appliances in the room.

Thermocouple!

Eventually, I’ve discovered it was the thermocouple. The joint between the thermocouple and the rest of the wire is broken inside the heatshrink. So when the X position exceeds soemthing like 100mm, a thermal runaway is prevented by rebooting the printer. I wish it would at least tell me, or something!

Soldering the thermocouple would change the resistance and the target temperatures could be very wrong. Thermocouples are cheap on Amazon, I’ll just get a few replacements. But… In the meantime, I want to print! So I’ve wrapped the wire around tightly, and electrical taped it… Seems to be OK..!
